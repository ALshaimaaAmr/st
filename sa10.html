Securing support from business


118 comments


In this step we think about why, and in what ways, businesses and corporates choose to support charities. We’ll then briefly consider what you will need in order to begin speaking with them about your cause.


Unlike the other givers and grant-makers that we have looked at so far in this course, businesses and companies tend not to formally publish their charitable or corporate social responsibility programmes. Thus, establishing which businesses are a fit for your cause or charity - and then establishing contact with them - is approached in much the same way as individual supporters and givers.


In the next section we’ll consider why individuals give, how they choose what charities and good causes to support, and how they can be asked for support. We’ll then go on to look at how to identify and map which formal organisations, individuals and businesses should form part of your fundraising mix.


Reference


Burlingame, D.F. & Young, D.R. (1996) Corporate Philanthropy at the Crossroads. Bloomington, IN: Indiana University Press.
