Welcome to this course


85command


Thank you for enrolling on our course on Dyslexia and Foreign Language Teaching. In this course you will gain a thorough understanding of how dyslexia and other specific learning differences affect learning an additional language.


You will become familiar with techniques to assist dyslexic students in successfully learning languages. The methods and techniques that we discuss in the course will also be applicable to teaching other academic subjects to second language speakers with dyslexia and other learning differences.


By the end of the course you will have a repertoire of teaching tasks and useful approaches to help dyslexic students to become successful language learners.


We also hope that this course will help you to become confident about how to create an inclusive learning environment for dyslexic students in your educational context.


Some of our steps and video lectures feature Spanish, Chinese and Arabic translations as well.


About the cours:


About the course


An introductory video in which dyslexic students talk about their experiences learning additional languages. These videos will help you reflect on how learners’ perspectives can inform your pedagogical choices.


A video lecture with an expert on dyslexia and language teaching that gives you a theoretical overview of the issues and practical guidance that you can take back to your classroom, covering:


-The effect of dyslexia on foreign language learning - Professor Judit Kormos, Lancaster University, United Kingdom


-Accommodating dyslexic students in the foreign language classroom - Dr Anne Margaret Smith, University of Cumbria, ELTwell, United Kingdom


-Developing phonological and orthographic awareness - Professor Joanna Nijakowska, University of Lodz, Poland


Conversations with experts - Professor Kate Cain, Lancaster University, United Kingdom, will discuss reading comprehension difficulties and how to develop children’s understanding of written texts with Professor Judit Kormos. Dr. Margaret Crombie will share ideas about how to help dyslexic students acquire modern foreign languages.


Reading materials for you to broaden your understanding


Tasks that ask you to apply what you learned to your own teaching context. You will evaluate language teaching tasks and other course material and design your own tasks.


At the end of the week, there will be an opportunity to look back on what you learned and think about how you can apply this new knowledge in your own context.
