Key Terms


Key Terms for Ethical AI Ethical AI: An approach to artificial intelligence that prioritizes moral considerations and human welfare in the development and application of AI technologies. Externality-first perspective: A viewpoint that prioritizes the effects of AI on humans and society, rather than focusing solely on economic or corporate benefits. Digital rights: Rights specific to the digital age, including privacy, consent for data collection, and control over biometric data. Critical thinking in tech: The application of analytical and evaluative thinking to examine claims and practices in the technology industry, particularly to counteract potential propaganda. Big tech: Large, influential technology companies that dominate the industry and often shape public discourse around technology. Human-centric technology: An approach to technological development that prioritizes human needs, well-being, and rights over purely economic or technological goals. Technological determinism: The idea that technology drives societal progress, which this course challenges by emphasizing that technology is a tool rather than a solution in itself. Economic model (in tech): The framework that describes how resources are allocated and used within the technology sector, which the course suggests needs reframing to better serve human interests. Natural rights: Fundamental rights inherent to human beings, often recognized during historical events like the French Revolution and further developed post-World War II, which may inform our understanding of rights in the digital age. Propaganda in tech: Misleading or biased information disseminated by technology companies or venture capitalists to promote their interests or products, often requiring critical analysis to identify and counteract.
