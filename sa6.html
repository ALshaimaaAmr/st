It should take you around 4 hours each week to complete the tasks in this course – this involves watching videos, joining discussions, reading articles and completing any exercises – but you may find yourself spending more time than this, if you do want to explore or discuss in more depth. There are also additional resources you can download or link to for future referral and use.


To get the most out of this course, do make use of the opportunities to interact with other learners using the comments on each step. Whilst we hope and expect that you will learn from the course articles, videos and practical exercises provided, there’s also a huge amount of learning from each other that goes on in the discussions, so please do comment where you can.


Once you’ve watched the introductory video from Lesley, please do get started with using the comments. You may wish to post a message to the comments on this page to introduce yourself. In your message you might include something about your interest in fundraising, what cause you want to raise money for, your level of fundraising experience and knowledge, and what you want or expect from this course. Please remember, you don’t need to post anything that you aren’t comfortable sharing online. Then take some time to browse through other learners’ messages in the comments and try replying to a few messages.


Facilitation


You can take the course at any time, but we will not always be available for feedback or ‘facilitiation’. For live tutor interaction, join the course 13 Jan - 3 Feb 2022.


A note on the course resources


We produced the course during the coronavirus lockdown, and so several of the course videos and resources have a rather ‘home-made’ feel to them! We hope you will enjoy these resources and appreciate that at this time we were restricted in what we were able to produce. When we are able, we do plan to update the resources for future runs of the course.
