Meet the team

Your lead educator on this course is Dr Claire Routley. Claire is a Postdoctoral Research Associate at the Centre for Philanthropy, with a particular interest in legacy and in memory giving. She also works as a fundraising consultant, specialising in legacy fundraising.

Joining Claire as a course educator is Stuart Chell. Stuart leads an agency that delivers fundraising campaigns and trains fundraisers for small and medium sized charities. His personal specialisms are high value giving and faith based fundraising. He is also currently undertaking postgraduate studies in the Centre for Philanthropy at the University of Kent.

Claire and Stuart will be supported by fellow educators Dr Ali Body, Director of Studies of the Masters of Philanthropic Studies and Dr Beth Breeze, Director of the Centre for Philanthropy.

The course was first put together by Dr Lesley Alborough, who researches and teaches gifting and asking practices in the Third Sector. Lesley presents many of the course videos.

Throughout the course we will be joined by expert practitioners in the field, and we’ll introduce them as they join us.

Other than at advertised times (see the course front page, emails or ‘pinned’ comments), the Educators won’t usually be able to join the discussions themselves or respond to individual comments, but the course encourages a strong learning community. The learning is focussed around debate and discussion - supporting other learners, sharing your own experience and knowledge, and listening to new perspectives. We hope that you will enjoy interacting with and learning from each other in this way. Don’t forget to comment, reply to other learners and ‘like’ comments.
